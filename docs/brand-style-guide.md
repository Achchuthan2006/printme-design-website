# PrintMe Brand Style Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how PrintMe should look, feel, and sound across the marketing site, product flows, account area, admin area, and future digital brand work.

The official logo in [public/printme-logo.svg](/C:/Users/Achch/Desktop/PrintMe/public/printme-logo.svg) is the primary brand anchor. The rest of the system should support that mark, not compete with it.

## Brand Personality

PrintMe should feel:

- bold
- clear
- practical
- locally credible
- premium without being flashy
- fast-moving but controlled
- professional and reassuring

PrintMe should not feel:

- generic startup
- luxury-for-luxury’s-sake
- futuristic
- playful or whimsical
- crowded
- trendy at the expense of readability

## Core Brand Traits

- `Direct`: clear actions, clear labels, clear expectations
- `Reliable`: strong hierarchy, trustworthy surfaces, no visual confusion
- `Local`: Scarborough presence and accessible support matter
- `Capable`: the site should suggest production knowledge, not just design polish
- `Efficient`: users should feel momentum toward quote, order, upload, or contact

## Logo Rules

### Primary Mark

- The official horizontal `PrintMe` wordmark is the primary brand mark.
- `Print` remains dark/near-black.
- `Me` remains brand orange-red.
- The wordmark should stay optically crisp with enough quiet space around it.

### Clearspace

- Minimum clearspace: at least the height of the `M` in `Me` on all sides.
- Never crowd the wordmark with borders, nav labels, icons, or tight CTA groupings.

### Sizing Guidance

- Header: use the shared `BrandLogo` `header` size variant.
- Footer: use the shared `BrandLogo` `footer` size variant.
- Do not manually resize the image in one-off page code unless there is a documented new variant.
- For tiny contexts such as browser icons, app icons, or favicons, use the dedicated compact mark from [app/icon.svg](/C:/Users/Achch/Desktop/PrintMe/app/icon.svg) instead of forcing the full horizontal wordmark.

### Logo Misuse To Avoid

- Do not stretch or condense the logo.
- Do not recolor the `Print` half to orange.
- Do not add outlines, gradients, glows, shadows, or decorative effects directly to the wordmark.
- Do not place it on busy backgrounds without enough contrast.
- Do not use the full wordmark at tiny favicon-scale sizes.
- Do not rotate, skew, crop, or place it inside decorative shapes.

### Brand Colors

- `brand.DEFAULT`: `#d94620`
- `brand.dark`: `#b73314`
- `brand.deep`: `#8f2813`
- `brand.light`: `#ef6a46`
- `brand.soft`: `#fff2ee`

### Core Neutrals

- `ink`: `#121110`
- `slate`: `#5f5b58`
- `line`: `#e7e2dd`
- `canvas`: `#f7f5f2`
- `panel`: `#ffffff`

### Color Intent

- Orange-red is the action and emphasis color.
- Near-black provides authority and anchors the brand to the `Print` half of the logo.
- Off-white and soft neutrals keep the interface premium without disconnecting from the strong logo contrast.
- Brand orange should be concentrated in CTAs, active emphasis, trust accents, and select highlights, not used as a wash everywhere.

## Typography

### Families

- UI/body font: `Aptos`, `Segoe UI Variable`, `Segoe UI`, `Helvetica Neue`, `Arial`, `sans-serif`
- Display font: `Arial Black`, `Segoe UI Variable`, `Segoe UI`, `Helvetica Neue`, `Arial`, `sans-serif`

### Rationale

- The logo is heavy, bold, and sans-driven.
- Display typography should echo that visual confidence rather than introducing a separate editorial personality.
- Body copy stays clean and readable, with enough warmth from spacing and tone rather than from decorative typography.

### Type Hierarchy

- `Display titles`: large, heavy, tight tracking, used for page and hero headlines
- `Section titles`: still bold and compact, but slightly more restrained than hero display
- `Eyebrows/kickers`: uppercase, small, brand-colored, used sparingly as structure
- `Body copy`: readable, calm, practical, 15px to 18px depending on surface
- `Button/CTA text`: uppercase, bold, directional

## Imagery Direction

- Use print-led visuals, mockups, paper surfaces, signage, printed materials, packaging, and realistic local-business contexts.
- Imagery should feel production-aware, not abstract tech-company stock.
- Keep product visuals clean, geometric, and implementation-friendly.
- Use dark surfaces and orange-red accents sparingly to echo the logo without making every image feel branded-by-overlay.

## Icon Style

- Icons are outline-based, rounded, and clean.
- Stroke weight should stay consistent across the interface.
- Icons should support utility and trust, not become decorative noise.
- Rounded containers and soft brand fills are preferred for icon framing on cards and trust strips.

## Tone Of Voice

### Voice Principles

- clear
- grounded
- capable
- reassuring
- local
- practical

### Voice Do

- explain what happens next
- reduce hesitation
- sound like a real local business with production experience
- be concise but not abrupt
- keep promises realistic

### Voice Avoid

- hype language
- empty “innovation” copy
- slang
- hard-sell urgency
- overly corporate abstraction

## Messaging Direction

- Lead with clarity and action.
- Reassure users that files, timing, and production details are reviewed before work begins.
- Emphasize local support, pickup confidence, and fast answers.
- Make PrintMe sound experienced and helpful, not complicated.
